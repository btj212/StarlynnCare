The first 90 days — mapped

A diagnosis doesn't tell you what to do tomorrow. This does.

Weeks 1–4 · Safety, legal, clinical

☐ Get a written copy of the diagnosis from the neurologist — you'll need this for legal steps
☐ Assess driving safety — this may need to happen now, not later
☐ Secure the home: stove knobs, door alarms, medication lockbox
☐ If no power of attorney exists, consult an elder law attorney immediately (capacity windows close)
☐ Review financial accounts — check for recent unusual activity
☐ Ask the diagnosing physician: what stage, and what changes should trigger reassessment?

Weeks 5–8 · Facility research (before urgency hits)

☐ Research 5–8 memory care facilities in your target area — before you need to move
☐ Look up each on StarlynnCare — read the inspection record, not the website copy
☐ Identify your payer type and confirm which facilities accept it
☐ Ask: does this person have behaviors (wandering, hallucinations, aggression) that require specialized units?
☐ Request admission agreement copies from your top 3 — read before any tour

Weeks 9–12 · Narrow, tour, decide

☐ Tour your top 2–3 facilities — bring the inspection record printout
☐ Ask about specific citations from the record — watch how they respond
☐ Check whether you're making this decision proactively (best time) or in crisis (still okay — just move faster)
☐ If still home: set the criteria that will trigger the move (document them so everyone agrees)
☐ Consider getting on a waitlist even before you're ready to move — the best facilities fill first
