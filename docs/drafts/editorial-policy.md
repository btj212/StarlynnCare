## Data sourcing

StarlynnCare grounds facility profiles in primary regulatory records from each state where profiles are live:

- **California:** CDSS Community Care Licensing deficiency narratives, complaint outcomes (where published), and facility characteristics matched to official RCFE license numbers. Records are ingested directly from CDSS exports; license identifiers link back to the CDSS FacDetail portal for independent verification.
- **Texas:** HHSC Long-Term Care licensing and inspection records, matched to ALF license numbers with links to the HHSC LTC Online Search portal.
- **Oregon:** Oregon DHS Long-Term Care Licensing inspection records for ALFs and RCFs. Memory Care Endorsement status is documented where published.
- **Washington:** DSHS ALF licensing and inspection records. Specialized Dementia Care contract status is noted where available via the DSHS public lookup.
- **Minnesota:** MDH Assisted Living Facility with Dementia Care licensing records under Chapter 144G, effective August 2021.

Editorial guides — cost benchmarks, glossaries, tour checklists, regulatory explainers — cite statute or recognized public datasets where numeric claims appear. Sourced footnotes appear inline on every guide page. StarlynnCare does not fabricate inspection outcomes, ratings, licensure status, or payer program details.

CMS Care Compare data may inform supplemental SNF context on comparison articles; it is labeled when present and not substituted for ALF inspection records.

## Grading methodology

Facility grades derive from peer comparison within each state's licensed facility population. A grade reflects how a facility's citation history compares to similarly-licensed facilities operating under the same regulatory framework — not against a national average. Full methodology is published at [/methodology](/methodology).

## What triggers a grade update

A facility's grade is recalculated when any of the following occur:

- A new inspection record is ingested from the state regulator's export
- A complaint investigation outcome is published by the regulator and adds to the citation record
- A licensing status change (suspension, revocation, change of ownership) is reflected in the source data
- The peer comparison cohort is materially changed (e.g., new state launch shifts the normalization baseline)

The footer on every profile page displays the date of the most recent data ingest. Families who see a discrepancy between what a facility told them and what the profile shows should check the primary regulator portal linked on the profile — if the regulator's record differs from ours, send a correction request to hello@starlynncare.com with the license number and the updated primary-source URL.

## Conflict of interest

StarlynnCare accepts no referral commissions, lead bonuses, or paid placement fees from facility operators. No operator can purchase a higher grade, a featured position, or adjacency to directory rankings. Sponsorship or promotional language does not appear adjacent to graded profiles.

Neither founder holds equity in any licensed memory care operator reviewed on this site. No commercial relationships with operators are pending. Future partnerships — research grants, foundation funding, or data licensing — will be disclosed on this page before they take effect if they could materially influence how facilities are graded or surfaced.

## Corrections

Factual corrections related to licensing data, addresses, cited inspection dates, or regulatory classifications are acknowledged within **five business days** of verifiable notice. Submit corrections to [hello@starlynncare.com](mailto:hello@starlynncare.com) with the facility's license number and a link to the primary-source document that contradicts the published copy.

Substantive clinical edits to editorial guides carry an updated reviewer date on the affected page. Opinion disagreements from operators are expected and do not trigger a correction. Factual disputes require primary-source documentation — a revised regulatory export, a published CCLD record, or equivalent state regulator output.
