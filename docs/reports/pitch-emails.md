For context: citation activity across California RCFEs in our dataset rose 132% from 2021 to 2024, tracking directly with the post-COVID resumption of in-person inspections. The compliance problems were there before the pandemic. They just weren't being documented at the same rate.

## Accuracy & Framing Notes

- All figures are from the StarlynnCare dataset of 484 California-licensed RCFEs. They are not claims about all California RCFEs.
- "Cited for the same violation in 3+ inspection visits" refers to the same CDSS regulation code appearing as a deficiency citation across distinct, dated inspection visit records.
- Opal Care LLC citation counts are from the published report and the underlying CDSS records. Do not describe current conditions — these are historical inspection records.
- The 132% figure is the change in citation rate from 2021 to 2024 within this dataset, consistent with documented post-COVID inspection resumption. Do not characterize this as proof of deteriorating care quality absent context.
- StarlynnCare is a data-journalism and consumer-transparency tool, not a rating agency. The framing in all versions reflects this.
- Stars Lin's RN credential and editorial review role is mentioned in Version A and Version C to establish clinical context. Do not claim she reviewed every facility record — she provides clinical interpretation of findings.
